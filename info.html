On Display 2016

Montreal, QC / January 21, 2016 at 5PM

#ondisplaymtl2016

Be ready

Bring your laptop or tablet to show your digital portfolio. No one likes to waste time: have your browser tabs open and your files ready so you can get the most out of the limited time with the judges.

Focus

Select your projects carefully to reflect the work you want to pursue. Don’t have enough to show? Include any relevant side-projects. Remember that quality always trumps quantity.

Find the appropriate format

How are your skills best presented? Embed gifs or videos to show your animation skills. Write a case study that demonstrates your problem solving abilities.

Flow and Consistency

Find the narrative thread and tell the story behind your projects. If you designed a sign-up flow, show it step by step.

Design rationale

Explain your process, and the thoughts behind your design decisions.

Run the extra mile

Identify areas you would like to work on and ask the judges to focus their feedback on it. Seize the chance to ask all of your burning questions.

Prepare your portfolio

Who is this portfolio review for?

On Display welcomes any student or junior designer who wants feedback in the following fields: UI and UX design, motion design, icon design, web design, and creative direction. If you’ve designed apps or websites, this is for you.

It’s not for you if you are a senior designer, a type designer, photographer, game designer, fashion designer or interior designer.

Is my portfolio strong enough for the event?

The purpose of On Display is to help you grow at the beginning of your design career. It’s a unique opportunity to learn from the best: don't hesitate and come get all the feedback!

Other questions?

Join our Slack channel to chat with organizers and fellow participants. You can also send questions our way; we’re happy to help!
